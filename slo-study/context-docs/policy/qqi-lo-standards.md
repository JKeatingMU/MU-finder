specificity criteria, and measurability guidance are elaborated; QQI endorses them
by reference rather than duplicating them.

**Research note:** The phrase "specific and sufficiently detailed" does not appear
verbatim in QQI documents. The operative QQI language is "explicitly specified",
"not vague, ambiguous or generalised", and "appropriate to award title, credit volumes".

## 1. Foundational Definition of Learning Outcomes

**QP 24, Glossary:**
> "Learning outcomes: A learner's knowledge, skill and competence change as a result
> of learning. Learning outcomes outline what a learner is expected to know, be able
> to do and understand at the end of a learning process."
> *(Citing: Cedefop, 2017)*

**QP 24, Section 2.3:**
> "All awards included within the Framework are based on recognised standards
> specified in terms of knowledge, skill or competence and expressed as learning
> outcomes. Learning outcomes are statements of what an enrolled learner is expected
> to know, understand and, where appropriate, demonstrate, on completion of a
> programme of education and training which leads to an award included within the
> Framework."

---

## 2. LOs Must Be Explicitly Specified at Module Level

**QP V.6 (2024 Draft), Criterion 2, Appendix 2, Indicators G and H:**
> "G. For each programme and embedded programme
> i. The MIPLOs [Minimum Intended Programme Learning Outcomes] and any other
> educational or training objectives of the programme are explicitly specified.
> ii. The MIPLOs are consistent with or exceed the relevant QQI awards standards.
> H. Where applicable, the minimum intended module learning outcomes (MIMLOs) are
> explicitly specified for each of the programme's modules."

**QP.17-V1.03 (2017), Criterion 17.2g:**
> "g. For each programme and embedded programme
> i. The minimum intended programme learning outcomes and any other educational or
> training objectives of the programme are explicitly specified.
> ii. The minimum intended programme learning outcomes to qualify for the QQI award
> sought are consistent with the relevant QQI awards standards.
> h. Where applicable, the minimum intended module learning outcomes are explicitly
> specified for each of the programme's modules."

---

## 3. LOs Must Align to NFQ Level

**QP 24, Section 18.1, Criteria C–F:**
> "C. Awards must be expressed in terms of minimum intended learning outcomes which
> are in keeping with the award standard and the NFQ descriptors.
> D. Minimum intended learning outcomes must be appropriate to the award title,
> credit volumes, relevant occupational standards, and proposed delivery method.
> E. Awards must be allocated a level on the NFQ that is in keeping with the award
> standard, the NFQ descriptors and the intended learning outcomes.
> F. Awards must be allocated a class of award that is in keeping with the award
> standard, the NFQ descriptors and the proposed learning outcomes."

---

## 4. LOs Must Be Plainly Written — Vagueness Is Explicitly Rejected

**Assessment and Standards (2022), Section 2.1 Principle 4(f):**
> "The intended programme and module learning outcomes and assessment strategies
> should be plainly written and communicated at the start of a programme, not only
> to learners but also to all those involved with teaching and assessment."

**QP.17-V1.03 (2017), General Warning:**
> "Generalised, non-programme-specific, vague, ambiguous, contradictory or evasive
> responses to the criteria by the provider or evaluators are unacceptable and may
> result in — the refusal of validation if they appear in the provider's evaluation
> report and — the rejection by QQI of an independent evaluation report."

---

## 5. LOs Must Be Assessable

**Assessment and Standards (2022), Section 2.1 Principle 2(a):**
> "Awards (including those made under delegated authority) are made and classified
> exclusively on the basis of criterion-referenced assessment of learning outcomes
> (knowledge, skill and competence)."

**Assessment and Standards (2022), Section 2.2.3:**
> "Student assessment procedures are expected to be designed to measure the
> achievement of the intended learning outcomes and other programme objectives (ESG).
> Assessment procedures are based on clearly expressed intended learning outcomes."

**QP V.6 (2024 Draft), Criterion 10, Indicator H:**
> "The programme's assessment strategy clarifies how the assessment process ensures
> the programme's minimum intended programme learning outcomes will be assessed."

---

## 6. Assessment Must Map to LOs (Constructive Alignment)

**Assessment and Standards (2022), Section 2.1 Principle 3(b):**
> "Effective assessment is consistent with, supportive of, and derived from the
> intended programme and module learning outcomes."

**QP V.6, Criterion 10, Indicator G:**
> "There is a satisfactory written programme assessment strategy for the programme
> as a whole and there are satisfactory module assessment strategies for any of its
> constituent modules. These strategies include documented grading rubrics / guides."

---

## 7. LOs Should Be Learner-Focused

**QP V.6 (2024 Draft), Criterion 4, Indicator G(ix):**
> "The programme title … is learner focused and meaningful to the learners."

**Assessment and Standards (2022), Section 2.2.3:**
> "Intended programme and module learning outcomes should be understood by learners
> and all those involved in teaching and assessment within the programme."

## Summary: QQI LO Quality Criteria (operative)

| Requirement | Source |
|---|---|
| Explicitly specified at programme and module level | QP V.6 Criterion 2-G/H; QP.17 17.2g/h |
| Expressed in terms of knowledge, skill and competence | QP 24 s.2.3; A&S s.2.1 |
| Consistent with NFQ descriptors and award standard | QP 24 s.18.1C; QP V.6 Criterion 2-D |
| Appropriate to award title and credit volume | QP 24 s.18.1D |
| Plainly written and communicated to learners | A&S s.2.1(4f) |

## Prompt-Ready Extract (for LLM evaluator)

```
IRISH QUALITY STANDARD — QQI (Quality and Qualifications Ireland):

Module learning outcomes (MIMLOs) must satisfy the following criteria (QP 24, 2024;
QP.17, 2017; Assessment and Standards, 2022):

1. EXPLICITLY SPECIFIED: Each module must have its minimum intended learning outcomes
   explicitly stated. Outcomes must not be vague, ambiguous, generalised, or evasive.
   [QP.17 Criterion 17.2h; QP V.6 Criterion 2-H; QP.17 general warning]

2. NFQ-ALIGNED: Outcomes must be consistent with the NFQ level descriptors and
   appropriate to the credit volume of the module.
   [QP 24 s.18.1C-D; QP.17 s.17.2g]

3. PLAINLY WRITTEN: Outcomes should be written clearly and communicated to learners
   and to all those involved in teaching and assessment.
   [Assessment and Standards 2022, s.2.1(4f)]

4. ASSESSABLE: Assessment is made exclusively on the basis of criterion-referenced
   assessment of learning outcomes. Assessment procedures must be designed to measure
   LO achievement.
   [Assessment and Standards 2022, s.2.1(2a) and s.2.2.3]

5. CONSTRUCTIVELY ALIGNED: Assessment strategies must be consistent with, supportive
   of, and derived from the module learning outcomes.
   [Assessment and Standards 2022, s.2.1(3b)]

QQI's normative reference for LO *writing* guidance is:
Cedefop (2017). Defining, writing and applying learning outcomes: a European handbook.
http://dx.doi.org/10.2801/566770
```
