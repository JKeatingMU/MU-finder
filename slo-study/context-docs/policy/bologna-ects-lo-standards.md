## Key Extracts

### Section 3.2 — Learning Outcomes

> "Learning outcomes are statements of what a learner knows, understands and is able
> to do on completion of a learning process. They are defined in terms of knowledge,
> skills and competence."

> "Learning outcomes should be expressed in terms of what the learner will be able
> to *demonstrate* on completion of a programme or unit of learning. They should
> not describe the learning process itself, nor the content to be covered."

> "Learning outcomes are written using active verbs that describe observable or
> measurable performance. Verbs such as *understand*, *know*, and *be familiar with*
> are discouraged as they describe internal cognitive states that cannot be directly
> assessed."

**LO quality implication:**
- "Understand" and "know" are explicitly identified as unsuitable verbs — direct
  support for the D1 Tier 0 classification.
- The requirement for *demonstrable* achievement directly grounds D5 (assessability).
- The student-as-agent framing ("what the learner will be able to demonstrate")
  directly grounds D3.

### Section 3.3 — Relationship between ECTS Credits and Learning Outcomes

> "ECTS credits are allocated on the basis of the learning outcomes of a unit of
> learning and the associated student workload. The workload indicates the time
> students typically need to complete all learning activities required to achieve
> the expected learning outcomes."

> "For one academic year of full-time study, 60 ECTS credits are allocated. One
> ECTS credit corresponds to 25–30 hours of student workload."

**LO quality implication for D4 (scope):**
- A 5 ECTS module implies 125–150 hours of student workload.
- An LO that could be achieved in one hour of reading is too narrow (sub-task level).
- An LO that would require a full year is too broad (programme-level).
- The ECTS credit load should inform the scope judgement in D4.

### Section 3.4 — Writing Learning Outcomes

> "Learning outcomes should be:
> - Student-centred: focused on what the learner will achieve, not on what will be taught
> - Specific and clear: unambiguous statements of achievement
> - Assessable: capable of being evaluated through appropriate assessment methods
> - Realistic: achievable within the time and resources allocated
> - Consistent: coherent with the level of the qualification and the ECTS credit allocation"

**Note:** This is the core normative checklist for D3 (student-centred), D4 (realistic/
appropriate scope), D5 (assessable), and D6 (consistent with level).

## Prompt-Ready Extract (for LLM evaluator)

```
EUROPEAN QUALITY STANDARD — Bologna Process / ECTS (2015):

Learning outcomes must be:
1. Student-centred: describe what the learner will be able to DEMONSTRATE, not what
   the module will cover. Verbs like "understand" and "know" are explicitly discouraged.
2. Specific and clear: unambiguous statements of observable achievement.
3. Assessable: capable of being evaluated through an appropriate assessment method.
4. Scoped appropriately to the ECTS credit load:
   - 5 ECTS ≈ 125–150 hours student workload
   - An LO should represent a meaningful portion of this workload, not a sub-task
     (too narrow) nor a programme-level goal (too broad).
5. Consistent with the NFQ/EQF level of the qualification.

Source: ECTS Users' Guide 2015, European Commission, Sections 3.2–3.4
```
