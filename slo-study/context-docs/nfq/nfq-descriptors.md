# NFQ Level Descriptors
## Source: Quality and Qualifications Ireland (QQI)
## National Framework of Qualifications (NFQ)
## Extracted: April 2026 — verify against current qqi.ie/nfq for any revisions

Each NFQ level is described along three strands: **Knowledge** (breadth and kind),
**Know-how and Skill** (range and selectivity), and **Competence** (context, role,
learning to learn, insight). The extracts below focus on the cognitive demand
relevant to LO quality evaluation (D6 calibration).

---

## Level 6 — Higher Certificate / Advanced Certificate

**Knowledge**
Broad range of knowledge of one or more fields. Mainly based on established facts and
theories. Some current developments in the field.

**Know-how and Skill**
Demonstrate a broad range of specialised skills, tools and techniques. Select from a
range of procedures to solve problems. Use some advanced skills to carry out tasks.

**Competence**
Act in a range of varied and specific contexts. Take responsibility for consistency of
self-understanding and behaviour. Exercise some initiative and independence in carrying
out defined activities. Learning to learn: evaluate own learning; identify and use
learning resources.

**LO quality implication for D6:**
LOs at Level 6 should require more than recall; application of standard methods in
familiar contexts is the expected performance level. LOs using Tier 0 verbs
(*understand, know*) are likely under-calibrated. LOs requiring synthesis of novel
solutions are likely over-calibrated.

---

## Level 7 — Ordinary Bachelor Degree

**Knowledge**
Broad and coherent knowledge of one or more fields. Based on established theory with
some current developments. Some knowledge of the boundaries of the field.

**Know-how and Skill**
Demonstrate a range of specialised skills and tools. Select from a range of procedures
to address problems, often unpredictable. Use advanced skills to carry out tasks and
to innovate.

**Competence**
Act in varied and specific contexts. Take responsibility for the nature and quality of
outputs. Exercise significant initiative and independence. Learning to learn: evaluate
own learning needs; develop own learning strategies.

**LO quality implication for D6:**
Level 7 LOs should move beyond application in familiar contexts to analysis and
judgement in less predictable situations. Tier 1 verbs (*describe, list*) may be
appropriate for foundational modules but represent under-calibration if used
consistently across a programme.

---

## Level 8 — Honours Bachelor Degree

**Knowledge**
Detailed knowledge and understanding of one or more fields. Includes current
developments and the nature and limits of the field. Some understanding of related fields.

**Know-how and Skill**
Demonstrate specialised technical, creative or conceptual skills. Identify and address
complex and non-routine problems. Use advanced skills to create or produce evidence of
learning.

**Competence**
Act in a range of professional and learning contexts. Take significant responsibility
for the work of others and for resourcing and managing processes. Exercise substantial
initiative and independence. Learning to learn: evaluate own and others' learning;
develop and manage own learning strategies and take responsibility for professional
development.

**LO quality implication for D6:**
The modal expectation at Level 8 is Tier 2–3: analysis, evaluation, synthesis,
critique, and design. LOs dominated by Tier 0–1 verbs represent a notable
calibration gap. The ability to address novel and complex problems is the key marker.

---

## Level 9 — Master's Degree

**Knowledge**
Specialised or advanced knowledge with critical understanding. Knowledge of the
boundaries and frontiers of a field. Understanding of the relationship between
knowledge in the field and other fields.

**Know-how and Skill**
Demonstrate mastery of specialised skills. Diagnose and address complex and
unpredictable problems requiring innovative solutions. Use advanced and specialised
skills to devise solutions.

**Competence**
Act with considerable autonomy and initiative in professional and equivalent contexts.
Manage complex technical or professional activities, including within teams.
Demonstrate substantial capacity for learning to learn and for professional development.

**LO quality implication for D6:**
Level 9 LOs should consistently demand Tier 2–3 performance. Critical evaluation,
synthesis of knowledge from multiple sources, and generation of original arguments
or solutions are expected. A preponderance of Tier 0–1 verbs at Level 9 is a strong
calibration signal.

---

## Level 10 — Doctoral Degree

**Knowledge**
Systematic understanding of a substantial body of knowledge which is at the frontier
of the field. Understanding of techniques applicable to own research or advanced
scholarship. Originality in the application of knowledge.

**Know-how and Skill**
Demonstrate the skills and techniques associated with advanced academic or professional
practice in the field. Exercise original thinking in solving problems in the field.

**Competence**
Communicate conclusions, knowledge and rationale clearly to specialist and non-specialist
audiences. Act with substantial autonomy. Take responsibility for making contributions
to knowledge and practice. Take responsibility for own and others' learning.

**LO quality implication for D6:**
Level 10 LOs in taught modules (as opposed to thesis requirements) should demand
Tier 3 precision: original synthesis, critique of frontier knowledge, design of
research instruments or methodologies. Any Tier 0 or Tier 1 verb usage at Level 10
is a calibration failure except in very specific foundational contexts.

## Summary Table (for prompt use)

| NFQ Level | Award | Minimum expected cognitive demand | Calibration signal |
|---|---|---|---|
| 6 | Higher Cert | Application in familiar contexts (Tier 1–2) | Tier 0 = under-calibrated |
| 7 | Ord. Bachelor | Analysis and judgement in varied contexts (Tier 2) | Tier 0–1 dominant = concern |
| 8 | Hons. Bachelor | Synthesis, evaluation, design (Tier 2–3) | Tier 0–1 dominant = gap |
| 9 | Master's | Critical evaluation, original solutions (Tier 3) | Tier 0–2 = calibration failure |
| 10 | Doctoral | Original knowledge generation (Tier 3) | Tier 0–1 = calibration failure |
