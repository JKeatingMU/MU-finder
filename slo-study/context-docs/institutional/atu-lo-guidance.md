# Atlantic Technological University — Institutional Guidance on Learning Outcomes

ATU operates under its **Programme Design Policy** (university-wide QA framework), standardising descriptors across legacy campuses (GMIT, IT Sligo, LYIT) using Bloom's Taxonomy. Tool: **Learning Outcomes Game Card**.

## Verb Requirements

ATU uses a "Learning Outcomes Game Card" framework based on Bloom's Taxonomy:

> "Try to start with a verb for immediate action"

Verbs are categorised under: Knowledge, Comprehension, Application, Analysis, Synthesis (original Bloom's taxonomy levels).

## NFQ and ECTS Requirements

> "Modules at ATU must be designed in multiples of 5 ECTS and must indicate the level on the NFQ at which the learning outcomes are associated."

## Module Descriptor Requirements

The Approved Programme Schedule (APS) requires each module descriptor to include:
- Aims
- Learning outcomes
- ECTS credits
- Indicative content
- Student contact hours
- Student effort/self-directed learning hours
- Assessment strategy

Workload standard: typically 20–25 hours of learner effort per ECTS credit.

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- Active verb (Bloom's) required — Game Card is the practical tool
- NFQ level must be assigned per module
- ECTS credit volume is part of the LO scope calibration
- ATU is a merged institution — LO quality may vary across legacy campuses (GMIT, IT Sligo, LYIT); apply consistent QQI/NFQ baseline
- No explicit prohibited term list found; apply general Irish HE standards
