# Marino Institute of Education — Institutional Guidance on Learning Outcomes

MIE degrees are accredited by **Trinity College Dublin (TCD)**. MIE adheres to QQI guidelines but defers specifically to **TCD's Bologna Desk guidance** for LO writing. Primary reference: Scattergood, J. (2008). *Writing learning outcomes at programme and module levels*. Trinity College Bologna Desk.

## Definition (verbatim, MIE Programme Handbooks)

> "Programme Learning Outcomes identify the range of essential knowledge and the nature of that knowledge, along with the necessary skills and attitudinal dispositions which must be achieved by practitioners within a profession."

(Citing Scattergood, 2008 — TCD Bologna Desk framework.)

## Assessment Alignment Requirements (verbatim, MIE Assessment Policy)

> "Assessment design should ensure that assessment modes and methodologies are valid, reliable and equitable and aligned with programme and module learning outcomes."

> "Questions should reflect the broad learning outcomes of the module, and not focus on obscure aspects to 'catch students out'."

LOs must be:
- Genuinely measurable
- Equitable across student groups
- Broad enough to guide assessment design rather than narrow enough to be gamed

## Institutional Context

- Teacher education college — LOs frequently address professional practice, pedagogical knowledge, and attitudinal dispositions (affective domain prominent)
- TCD Bologna Desk framework emphasises the Bologna Process conception of LOs: what a graduate is able to do, know, and demonstrate — aligned with ECTS Users' Guide 2015

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- TCD/Scattergood framework is the operative LO writing standard — broadly consistent with Kennedy 2006 and Bologna Process conventions
- Affective domain LOs (skills and attitudinal dispositions) are appropriate and expected in teacher education — do not flag professional disposition statements as vague where context is clear
- Assessment alignment is an explicit institutional requirement — LOs should be broad enough to support valid, reliable assessment design
- "Obscure aspects" framing: LOs that are too narrow or too specific may be as problematic as vague ones in the MIE context
- Apply QQI/NFQ baseline for verb observability; TCD Bologna Desk guidance is consistent with Cedefop (2017)
