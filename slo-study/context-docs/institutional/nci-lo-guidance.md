# National College of Ireland — Institutional Guidance on Learning Outcomes

NCI bases module and programme LO structures entirely on **QQI/NFQ award descriptors**, mapping every outcome to the Knowledge, Skill, and Competence strands. Source: RPEL (Recognition of Prior Experiential Learning) Guidelines and internal module descriptor standards.

## NFQ Alignment (verbatim)

> "The detail and type of knowledge described should reflect the Level you are writing at. The more facts and concepts that are layered on top of each other and integrated, the higher the level of learning is considered to be."

## Competence and Selectivity Standard (verbatim)

> "The skill of selectivity refers to the consideration that the learner exercises in carrying out tasks, through selecting from the range of know-how and skills available... For example, acting effectively and autonomously in complex or ill-defined and unpredictable situations requires higher levels of learning than well-defined and predictable situations."

This links cognitive demand directly to the NFQ competence descriptors: higher NFQ levels require demonstrated selectivity and autonomous judgement in unpredictable contexts.

## Guidance Approach

NCI is more prescriptive about NFQ level calibration than verb selection per se. The primary quality criterion is that the LO's cognitive demand matches the NFQ level — with complexity, integration, and autonomy scaling upward through the levels.

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- NFQ level calibration is the primary standard — assess whether the cognitive demand of the LO matches the NFQ level of the module
- Higher NFQ levels require: integration of concepts, selectivity, autonomous action in ill-defined situations
- Lower NFQ levels: well-defined, predictable tasks with defined procedures
- NCI does not have a separate verb list; apply QQI/Kennedy 2006 baseline for verb observability
- Knowledge / Know-How & Skill / Competence strand mapping is expected at programme level
