# Institute of Art, Design and Technology Dún Laoghaire — Institutional Guidance on Learning Outcomes

IADT has a dedicated **Guide to Writing Learning Outcomes** (Teaching & Learning unit). Uses Bloom's Taxonomy but tailored to arts, technology, and design pedagogy. Explicitly maps to QQI Knowledge/Skill/Competence strands.

## Prohibited Terms (verbatim)

> "As much as possible you should avoid using the words such as 'know', 'understand' or 'appreciate' as these tend to be vague."

Stem: LOs should be "followed by action verbs so that students are able to demonstrate that they have learned and hence achieved the outcomes."

## Verb Requirements — Level Progression (verbatim)

> "For instance, in an introductory module... the learning outcomes may require the students to be able to **define, recall, list, describe, explain or discuss**.
>
> For a more advanced module... the learning outcomes may require the students to be able to **formulate, appraise, evaluate, estimate or construct**."

Cognitive demand is expected to rise with module level — introductory modules use lower Bloom's levels; advanced modules require higher-order verbs.

## QQI Strand Mapping

IADT maps LOs to the QQI Knowledge/Skill/Competence strands:
- **Breadth:** How extensive is the learner's knowledge?
- **Kind:** What nature or quality of knowing has the learner engaged in?
- **Range:** How extensive are the skills?

This is a more structured alignment to QQI's framework than most institutions require at module level.

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- 'know', 'understand', 'appreciate' are explicitly prohibited — flag these
- Verb level should match module level: introductory → lower Bloom's; advanced → higher Bloom's
- QQI strand coverage (Knowledge / Know-How & Skill / Competence) is an IADT requirement
- Arts/design context: creative production verbs (formulate, construct, appraise) are appropriate at higher levels — do not penalise arts-domain verb choices that are genuinely evaluative or creative
- IADT's arts focus means affective and psychomotor domain verbs may appear; treat these as appropriate where the module context supports them
