## Definition (verbatim)

> "Learning Outcomes are clear statements of learning achievements for students, stating what it is the student should be able to demonstrate at the end of a period of learning."

## Standard Stem

- Module: **"On successful completion of this module, the student should be able to:"**
- Programme: **"On successful completion of this programme, the graduate will be able to:"**

## Quantity Requirements

- Module: **4–8 outcomes** (CELT guide); "In general a Module should have 5 or less learning outcomes" (AKARI system)
- Programme: **5–10 outcomes**

## Core Module LO Criteria — LOs should be:

- General enough to describe **essential learning**
- Specific enough to be **measurable**
- Clearly **focused on the learner**
- **Easy to understand** from the student's perspective
- **Linked (aligned)** to teaching and learning activities
- **Aligned to assessment**
- **Assessed at least once** during a programme
- **Performance-oriented**: "Ideally, they will be performance orientated"

Key question (verbatim): *"What is the essential learning for this module? Bear in mind that module learning outcomes should be capable of being assessed. How will students demonstrate their learning?"*

## The 8-Point "ACTIVE" Checklist (verbatim)

| Criterion | Meaning |
|---|---|
| Active | State what students will be able to **do** |
| Attractive | Students will **want** to achieve them |
| Comprehensible | Students will **know** what they mean |
| Appropriate | To the **level** of learning required for this course |
| Attainable | Most students will meet them with due effort |
| Assessable | Their achievement can be assessed/measured |
| Visible | Included in the course booklet and VLE |
| Aligned | To teaching, learning and assessment activities |

## Verb Guidance and Bloom's Taxonomy

Galway does not mandate a specific verb list. CELT recommends consulting learning taxonomies: **Bloom (1956), Biggs & Collis (1982) [SOLO], or Fink (2003)**. The Bloom's Taxonomy Task-Construction Wheel (St. Edward's University) is reproduced as the primary practical tool.

Sample verbs from the wheel:
- **Knowledge:** Define, Describe, Identify, Label, Memorise, Name, Recognise, State
- **Comprehension:** Change, Illustrate, Paraphrase, Restate, Transform
- **Application:** Apply, Classify, Discover, Interpret, Make, Model, Modify, Prepare
- **Analysis:** Analyse, Categorise, Compare, Contrast, Differentiate, Examine, Investigate
- **Synthesis:** Combine, Create, Design, Formulate, Hypothesise, Invent, Organise, Plan
- **Evaluation:** Appraise, Assess, Critique, Judge, Recommend, Summarise, Weigh

## NFQ Alignment — Institutional Requirement (binding, QA050)

> "A statement of Aims of the Programme and a list of intended Programme Level Learning Outcomes clearly aligned with the NFQ indicators."

From Programme Outcomes guide:
> "It is a **university requirement** that programmes be mapped on to the National Framework of Qualifications and therefore programme outcomes should be written to align to the NFQ level descriptors."

Galway acknowledges the cultural complexity: *"They use a language and approach that is not culturally embedded within the university sector per se, but yet if we claim a given NFQ level for a programme, we should be able to justify it in these terms for compliance."*

The full NFQ Grid of Level Indicators (all 10 levels, 8 strands) is reproduced as Appendix 5 of QA050. Module NFQ level required in AKARI: "usually 8, 9 or 10" for university modules.

## Change Governance (QA050)

- Changing module LOs = **minor change** → College curriculum committee approval
- Changing programme LOs = **major change** → University Curriculum & Programme Board approval

## Programme LO Notes

- Programme LOs are **not directly assessed** — indirectly demonstrated through module LO achievement
- Course map (programme outcome × module matrix) is the required tool
- LOs must appear in course booklet and VLE (Moodle/Canvas)
- Not a compilation: "It is important to ensure that programme learning outcomes are not simply a compilation of module or course outcomes"
- Stakeholder consultation required before finalising LOs (students, employers, programme team)
- LOs visible on website and course prospectus

## Operational System

LOs entered individually into **AKARI** (`nuig.akarisoftware.com/curriculum`), one per text field, with linkage to specific assessments. Changes require versioning and approval workflows.

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- Performance-orientation is the primary standard
- 4–8 per module (flag outliers; AKARI target is ≤5)
- NFQ alignment is a hard institutional policy requirement
- Each LO must be capable of being assessed
- Active verb required; Bloom's taxonomy is the reference (not mandated per-level)
- No explicit prohibited term list (unlike UCC/DCU) but non-observable verbs contradict the assessability criterion
- External references: QQI/NFQ + Bologna Process + UK QAA benchmark statements (no Cedefop reference found)
