# University of Limerick — Institutional Guidance on Learning Outcomes

Produced by the **UL Centre for Teaching and Learning**. Document: *Writing Learning Outcomes: A guide for academics*.

## Guidance Approach

UL has a dedicated institutional guide based on Bloom's Taxonomy, explicitly grounded in NFQ level indicators. Less prescriptive than UCC/DCU in terms of prohibited terms, but mandates verb-based LO writing throughout.

## Verb Requirements

Bloom's Taxonomy is mandated for outcome writing. Verbatim staff reflection from guidance:

> "I have also learnt through this process the importance of using the appropriate action verbs when writing learning outcomes."
> "The use of Bloom's taxonomy helped with this process as it provided a hierarchy of ways students can demonstrate their understanding."

## Prohibited/Discouraged Terms

Guidance warns specifically against:

> "vague and immeasurable outcomes"

No explicit banned word list found, but the Bloom's taxonomy framework implicitly excludes non-observable verbs (understand, know, etc.).

## NFQ Alignment

NFQ level is the primary calibration reference:

> "It was important to incorporate learning outcomes from all modules on the programme – it was also important to clarify the level of student understanding/knowledge/skills... for the MBS in HRM, this level would be level 9."

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- Active, observable verb required (Bloom's taxonomy reference)
- NFQ level is the primary calibration standard
- "Vague and immeasurable outcomes" are explicitly flagged as a problem
- Less prescriptive than UCC/DCU on count, stem, or prohibited terms — apply general Irish HE standards where UL-specific guidance is absent
