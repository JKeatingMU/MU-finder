# TU Dublin — Institutional Guidance on Learning Outcomes

TUD maintains strict internal Quality Assurance procedures for module descriptor formatting, requiring direct mapping to QQI/NFQ templates. Guidance drawn from the **Programme Validation Checklist** and module descriptor standards.

## Definition (verbatim)

> "Learning outcomes are the explicit written goals for each course within a programme. They indicate what the student should know and be able to do after completing the course."

## NFQ Alignment Requirements

Module descriptors must include:
- "Programme Learning Outcomes using relevant NFQ award descriptor template"
- "Mapping of Module Learning Outcomes to NFQ Award Descriptor Fields"

Each LO must be explicitly mapped to an NFQ award descriptor field (Knowledge, Know-How & Skill, or Competence strand).

## Module Descriptor Requirements

Each module descriptor requires:
- Contact hours and ECTS weighting
- Assessment breakdown percentages
- Each assessment component explicitly mapped to specific LOs
- No "unnecessary duplication" of LOs across assessment mapping

## Guidance Approach

TUD operates primarily through QA compliance rather than a standalone LO writing guide. The validation checklist enforces NFQ template adherence. No separate verb list or prohibited terms document found — TUD defaults to QQI/NFQ level descriptor language as the primary standard.

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- NFQ award descriptor mapping is the primary institutional standard — LOs should be classifiable against Knowledge / Know-How & Skill / Competence strands
- Assessment alignment is formally required (each LO must map to at least one assessment)
- TUD has a large PDF-sourced corpus (6 programmes) — LOs from those PDFs predate the TU merger; quality may reflect legacy IT Dublin/DIT standards
- Apply QQI/NFQ standards as the primary evaluative frame; TUD does not have its own supplementary verb guidance
