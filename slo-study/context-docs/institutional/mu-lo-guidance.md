# Maynooth University — Institutional Guidance on Learning Outcomes

MU integrates LO guidance into its **Postgraduate Diploma in Higher Education Teaching, Learning and Assessment (PDHETL)** modules and defers to QQI/NFQ for curriculum specifications. The institutional framing is drawn from *Curriculum as an Encounter* (MU research) and Teaching and Learning Guidelines.

## Verb Requirements

> "The important word in every learning outcome is the action verb, as this indicates the pedagogical decisions the teacher can make, the learning experience the student will have, and how the learning will be assessed."

Example: using "explain" to mean "give a detailed account" — the verb carries the cognitive demand signal.

## NFQ Alignment

Academic staff are required to:

> "write programme and module learning outcomes that adhere to recognised conventions, are aligned, and appropriate to learners' level and stage."

## Prohibited Terms / Restrictions

No explicit banned word list, but MU policy aligns with AHEAD/QQI guidelines:

> A Learning Outcome "needs to be designed so that it identifies WHAT needs to be done rather than specifying HOW [it] is to be done" — to avoid prescriptive barriers and ensure accessibility.

This frames over-specification (naming specific software, specific theories where alternatives exist) as a problem — complementary to the under-specification problem of vague verbs.

## Corpus Observations (from MU module data)

- System enforces a maximum of 8 LOs per module; no minimum enforced
- Mean LOs per module in MU corpus: ~5.9 (12,603 LOs / 2,139 modules)

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- Action verb is the central requirement — verb carries pedagogical and assessment signal
- NFQ level alignment expected
- MU guidance is less prescriptive than UCC/DCU at module level; PDHETL framework is for staff development rather than a module-writing checklist
- The WHAT-not-HOW principle guards against over-restriction as well as vagueness
- Apply general Irish HE standards (QQI/Cedefop/Kennedy 2006) where MU-specific guidance is thin
