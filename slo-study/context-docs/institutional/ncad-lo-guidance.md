# National College of Art and Design — Institutional Guidance on Learning Outcomes

NCAD is a recognised college of UCD and aligns with UCD's academic frameworks and QQI standards. Arts-focused LO conventions use a distinctive capitalised meta-verb structure.

## Distinctive LO Format (verbatim examples from module descriptors)

NCAD uses **capitalised meta-verbs** as LO headers, followed by contextual elaboration:

> "1. RESEARCH: Demonstrate an expanded understanding of the range and style of forms of writing relevant to contemporary art."
>
> "2. ANALYSE: Assess how specific forms of writing operate within a specific cultural or professional context..."

The capitalised verb names the overarching competence domain; the sentence that follows specifies the observable performance.

## LO Count and Assessment Mapping

- Typical LO count: **3–5 per module**
- Assessment mapping is strictly required: a table mapping every assessment tool directly to specific LOs
- RPL provision: "Where a student can demonstrate that they have achieved at least 80% of the learning outcomes of this module... they can apply to the School for that prior learning to be recognised."

## Alignment

- UCD academic frameworks (primary awarding body)
- QQI standards (regulatory compliance)
- Arts and creative practice conventions

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- NCAD's capitalised meta-verb format (RESEARCH:, ANALYSE:, EVALUATE:) is a deliberate stylistic convention — the observable performance is in the elaborating sentence, not the capitalised label alone
- Do not flag the capitalised label as a vague verb if the elaborating sentence contains a specific, measurable performance statement
- 3–5 LOs per module is the institutional norm
- Arts/creative context: verbs like "formulate", "construct", "appraise", "critique" are appropriate and should not be penalised
- Assessment mapping table is a required institutional element — flag if LOs appear unevaluable
- UCD's 6 Golden Rules and constructive alignment framework apply as the parent institution's standard
