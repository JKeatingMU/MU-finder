# Griffith College Dublin — Institutional Guidance on Learning Outcomes

Griffith College maintains strict standardisation through Academic and Quality Assurance boards. Distinctive feature: explicit use of **MIMLO** (Minimum Intended Module Learning Outcomes) and **MIPLO** (Minimum Intended Programme Learning Outcomes) terminology — directly reflecting QQI QP.17 language.

## Standard Stem (verbatim)

> "On successful completion of this module, learners are able to:"

Followed by numbered, verb-first outcomes: (i) Discuss... (ii) Define... (iii) Identify... (iv) Explain...

## LO Count

- **4–6 LOs per 5 ECTS module** — explicitly stated and enforced

## MIPLO Alignment (verbatim)

Every module descriptor includes a mandatory section:

> "Rationale for inclusion of the module in the programme and its contribution to the overall MIPLOs"

This enforces top-down curriculum design: module LOs must be justified against programme-level outcomes.

## Verb Requirements

Active verbs mandated throughout. Standard examples from descriptors: Discuss, Define, Identify, Explain. No explicit prohibited term list found, but active verb requirement implicitly excludes "know", "understand", etc.

## Corpus Notes

GC corpus was extracted from 74 PDFs (63 parseable modules, 441 LOs). Module codes not published in PDFs — moduleCode is null throughout the GC scored data. The "Module Objectives" section header was the parse target, not "Learning Outcomes" — Griffith PDFs use "Objectives" as the equivalent section.

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- MIMLO terminology is GC's standard — treat as equivalent to "module learning outcomes"
- 4–6 per module is the institutional norm; flag significant outliers
- Module-to-programme alignment (MIPLO mapping) is a formal requirement — LOs should be broad enough to connect upward to programme outcomes
- Active verb required; apply Kennedy 2006 / QQI QP.17 standards for observability
- GC's "Module Objectives" section is functionally equivalent to "Learning Outcomes" in other institutions
- D1 mean for GC is 1.914 (highest in corpus), Tier 0 rate ~0.5% — GC LOs are generally well-formed
