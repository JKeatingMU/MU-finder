## Standard Stem

**"On completion of this module, students will be able to…"**

## The 6 Golden Rules (verbatim where available)

- **Rule 4:** "Ensure all outcomes match what is taught, how it is taught, what work is set for students and how this work is marked." (constructive alignment — Biggs & Tang 2007 explicitly cited)
- **Rule 5:** "Involve students in the drafting and refining of learning outcomes."
- **Rule 6:** "Regard outcomes as general performance indicators that guide the judgement of academic assessors rather than definitive threshold statements."
- Rules 1–3 (from surrounding guidance): write from the student's perspective; use an active observable verb; limit outcomes to what can be assessed.

## Core LO Writing Principles

- **Verb requirement:** "Use action verbs when writing the outcomes to show what students will be able to know and do."
- **Observability:** Outcomes must be observable and/or measurable.
- **Constructive alignment:** Explicitly named as "the most fundamental principle of design in the outcomes-based curriculum that is used in UCD" — LOs must align with teaching/learning activities and assessment.
- **LOs as performance indicators, not thresholds:** "general performance indicators that guide the judgement of academic assessors rather than definitive threshold statements."

## Bloom's Revised Taxonomy (UCD primary framework)

UCD uses **Anderson et al. (2001)** Revised Taxonomy:
- Cognitive domain: Remember → Understand → Apply → Analyse → Evaluate → Create
- Knowledge dimension: Factual / Conceptual / Procedural / Metacognitive
- Affective domain (Krathwohl): Receiving → Responding → Valuing → Organisation (particularly applicable in Arts and Human Sciences)
- Psychomotor domain: Dave (1970) / Ferris & Aziz (2005)

Verbs like *understand*, *know*, *appreciate*, *be familiar with* are implicitly excluded by the active-verb requirement.

## QQI/NFQ Relationship

UCD is a **recognised awarding body** under the NFQ. Programme LOs must align with the NFQ and Irish Register of Qualifications. UCD publishes its own **Academic Level Descriptors** (approved October 2005) mapping UCD's internal level coding onto NFQ levels.

UCD adds supplementary guidance above QQI's minimum:

| Requirement | QQI (NFQ) minimum | UCD supplementary |
|---|---|---|
| LO format | Knowledge/skill/competence per level | Explicit stem: "On completion… students will be able to…" |
| Verb standard | Not specified | Active, observable verb; Bloom's Revised Taxonomy verb lists |
| Alignment | Not mandated | Constructive alignment mandatory (Biggs & Tang cited) |
| Student involvement | Not required | Golden Rule 5: students involved in drafting |
| LOs as thresholds | QQI: pass/fail thresholds | UCD: "general performance indicators… not definitive threshold statements" |
| Domains | Knowledge/skill/competence | Cognitive + affective + psychomotor explicitly differentiated |

## Module Descriptor Requirement (Academic Regulations s.3.14)

Module descriptor is a **required, publicly published document**. LOs are a mandatory element. "Pending" status flags missing mandatory data. Published to UCD Course Search each June.

## Notes for AI Evaluator

- Active observable verb required; Bloom's Revised Taxonomy is the reference framework
- Constructive alignment with assessment is a stated institutional requirement
- LOs are performance indicators, not threshold statements — avoid over-literal reading
- Student-centredness ("On completion… students will be able to…") required
- NFQ level alignment required at programme level
- No explicit LO count per module found — UCD guidance focuses on quality criteria not quantity bounds
