1 Overview

MU Programme Finder is a browser-based progressive web app (PWA) that helps prospective students explore the 55 MU undergraduate programmes for 2026. It requires no login, no server, and collects no personal data. It works fully offline once loaded and can be installed to a device home screen.

2 The Three Pathways

The app offers three independent routes into the programme data, accommodating students at different stages of decision-making.

Pathway 1
Personality Profile

For students who are unsure what to study — maps interests and cognitive strengths to programmes.

Pathway 2
Subject Explorer

For students who know their LC subjects — calculates estimated CAO points and shows achievability.

Pathway 3
Career Finder

For students who know what career they want — browses 37 paths and maps them to MU programmes.

2.1 Personality Profile

A psychometric-style quiz that maps a student's cognitive interests and strengths onto MU programmes.

Flow:

  1. Student selects a faculty (Arts / Science & Engineering / Social Sciences)
  2. Answers 9–12 Likert-scale statements (Strongly Disagree → Strongly Agree)
  3. Results screen shows a personalised strength profile bar chart, descriptions of top strengths, and all faculty programmes ranked by match percentage

Question sets:

  • Arts — 9 questions across Creative, Humanities, and Language
  • Science & Engineering — 12 questions across Scientific, Quantitative, Computing, and Creative
  • Social Sciences — 12 questions across Social, Business, Language, and Quantitative

The 8 strength categories:

CategoryWhat it measures
CreativeArtistic expression, design thinking, original making
HumanitiesCulture, history, philosophy, critical interpretation
LanguageCommunication, writing, debate, cross-cultural exchange
ScientificNatural world curiosity — biology, chemistry, physics, environment
QuantitativeMathematics, statistics, data, logical modelling
ComputingSoftware, algorithms, digital systems, coding
SocialPeople-centred motivation — wellbeing, rights, community
BusinessStrategy, markets, organisations, entrepreneurship

Match score formula:

matchScore = (primaryScore + secondaryScore)
            / ((primaryQuestions + secondaryQuestions) × 5)
            × 100

A category is only declared a "strength" if it scores at least 2 points above the next highest category. Balanced profiles (all categories broadly equal) display a neutral message rather than falsely declaring a top strength.

2.2 Subject Explorer

Calculates a student's estimated CAO points from their Leaving Certificate subjects and grades, then shows all MU programmes sorted by achievability.

Flow:

  1. Student selects up to 7 LC subjects from a grouped list (Languages, Mathematics, Sciences, Technology & Design, Business & Social, Humanities & Other)
  2. Selects a grade for each (Higher H1–H7 / Ordinary O1–O6 / Foundation F1–F2 for Maths and Irish)
  3. App calculates CAO points (best 6 subjects + Higher Maths bonus) and displays all 55 programmes with status indicators

2.3 Career Finder

Students browse or search 37 career paths across 8 groups and see the MU programmes most relevant to each career.

Career groups: Technology & Computing · Science & Research · Healthcare & Wellbeing · Business & Finance · Education & Social Work · Arts, Media & Communication · Humanities & Culture · Engineering & Design

3 Programme Directory

A browsable, searchable directory of all 55 MU 2026 undergraduate programmes, accessible directly from the Welcome screen.

  • Search — by programme title, CAO code, or keyword in description
  • Faculty filter — All / Arts / Science & Engineering / Social Sciences
  • Sort — by CAO code, title A–Z, points high–low, points low–high
  • Read more / Show less — inline expand of the programme description with CSS fade
  • Full summary — opens a modal with the complete prospectus description
  • CAO points chip — faculty-coloured, with tooltip confirming “2025 Round 1 (last round)”
  • MU page link — direct link to the programme’s page on maynoothuniversity.ie
  • Category pills — strength categories tagged to each programme
  • AI summary badge — shown where description was AI-generated (5 programmes)
  • Accessibility font toggle — three text sizes (small / normal / large), persisted across sessions

4 Saving & Comparing

A heart icon appears on the top-right of every programme card across all screens. Saved programmes persist across sessions via localStorage. The count is shown in the app header and links to the Programme Directory filtered to saved items.

Programme Comparison

Available when the Saved filter is active. Students select 2 or 3 saved programmes using the compare icon (alongside the heart). A sticky bar at the bottom shows the selection count; "Compare →" opens a side-by-side modal.

5 Deployment Contexts

The app is a fully static build — no server, no database, no runtime dependencies. The compiled output (the dist/ folder) can be copied to any web host or served directly from a file system.

iPad Kiosk — Open Day
  • Install the PWA via Safari → Share → Add to Home Screen for a fullscreen, app-like experience
  • Enable iOS Guided Access (Settings → Accessibility → Guided Access) to lock the device to the app for the duration of the Open Day
  • Load the app over Wi-Fi before the event; the service worker caches the app shell for fully offline use thereafter
  • The MU logo is loaded from the MU CDN — a local fallback copy should be added to public/ for fully offline kiosk use
  • No login, no personal data, no student-facing account required
  • Large touch targets and smooth animations are optimised for iPad use
MU Website — Admissions Pages
  • The live app is available at: jkeatingmu.github.io/MU-finder/
  • Can be linked directly from admissions or undergraduate study pages
  • Can be embedded in an <iframe> within the MU CMS if required
  • Can be self-hosted on the MU web server by copying the dist/ folder — no server-side processing required
  • Fully mobile-responsive and tested on Chrome, Safari, Edge, and Firefox
  • Students can install the PWA from any HTTPS page (browser will prompt automatically on Android/Chrome; Share → Add to Home Screen on iOS Safari)
  • If self-hosting, update base in vite.config.ts to match the server path, then rebuild
Recommended for MU web team: self-host the dist/ folder on the MU web server at a path such as maynoothuniversity.ie/programme-finder/. This removes the dependency on GitHub Pages and keeps the tool under the MU domain, which improves trust signals and allows a custom Content Security Policy to be set (see Security section).

Keeping it up to date

Any change made in the codebase and pushed to the main branch of the GitHub repository (github.com/JKeatingMU/MU-finder) triggers an automatic rebuild and redeploy via GitHub Actions. The live site updates within approximately 2 minutes. For MU self-hosted deployments, copy the freshly built dist/ folder to the web server after each update.

6 Progressive Web App

The app is a fully installable PWA requiring no app store. Installation steps:

  1. Open the app URL in a browser
  2. Use the browser’s “Add to Home Screen” / “Install” option
  3. The app installs like a native app and works offline
  • PWA manifest: public/manifest.json
  • Service worker: public/service-worker.js — caches the app shell on install
  • Theme colour: MU maroon #6b1a2b

9 Updating Content

Adding or editing programmes

Edit /src/data/courses.ts. Each entry follows this structure:

{
  id:                'mh101',
  code:              'MH101',
  title:             'The Maynooth Bachelor of Arts Degree',
  description:       'Short preview (shown in cards)',
  fullSummary:       'Full prospectus text (shown in modal)',
  primaryCategory:   'Humanities',
  secondaryCategory: 'Language',
  faculty:           'arts',
  points:            '300',
  url:               'https://www.maynoothuniversity.ie/arts',
  aiSummary:         false,
}

Updating quiz questions

Edit /src/data/questions.ts. Questions are grouped by faculty (artsQuestions, scienceQuestions, socialQuestions). Each question maps to one of the 8 strength categories.

Adding careers

Edit /src/data/careers.ts. Each career requires a group, primaryCategory, and optionally secondaryCategory.

Updating LC subjects

Edit /src/data/lcSubjects.ts. The facultyHint field controls which faculty is suggested when a student selects that subject.

Updating CAO points

Edit the points field in /src/data/courses.ts for each programme annually after CAO Round 1 results are published.

10 Security Review

The following review was conducted against the application source code and deployment configuration. As a client-side-only static application with no user accounts and no server component, the attack surface is significantly smaller than a typical web application. No significant vulnerabilities were identified.

AreaFindingStatus
Cross-Site Scripting (XSS) All user input (search field) is used only for string comparison (.toLowerCase().includes()) and is never rendered as HTML. React JSX escapes all dynamic content by default. No dangerouslySetInnerHTML usage found. Pass
Data injection All programme, career, and question data is baked into the JavaScript bundle at build time from static TypeScript files. No runtime API calls are made and no external data is fetched or parsed. Pass
localStorage usage Three keys are written: saved programme IDs (mu-favourites), font size preference (mu-dir-font), and a help-seen flag (mu-help-seen). No personally identifiable information is stored. Data never leaves the device. Pass
External links All links to external pages (maynoothuniversity.ie, mu.ie) use target="_blank" rel="noopener noreferrer", preventing tab-napping and referrer leakage. Pass
External image resource The MU logo is loaded from the MU CDN (maynoothuniversity.ie) using referrerPolicy="no-referrer". This creates a soft dependency on an external domain; a locally hosted copy is recommended for kiosk use. Note
HTTPS Enforced by GitHub Pages. The service worker will not register on non-HTTPS origins (browser restriction). Self-hosted deployments must be served over HTTPS. Pass
Service worker Caches the app shell only (index.html, manifest.json). No sensitive data is cached. Old cache versions are deleted on activation. The service worker scope is correctly scoped to the app path. Pass
GDPR / Data Protection No personal data is collected, transmitted, or stored server-side. No cookies, no analytics, no tracking. localStorage data is functional only and remains on the user’s device. No DPIA or consent mechanism is required for this use pattern. Pass
Content Security Policy (CSP) GitHub Pages does not support custom HTTP response headers, so no CSP is currently set. This is a common limitation of static hosting. If self-hosted on the MU web server, a CSP header should be configured to restrict script and style sources to the application origin. Recommend
Dependency security All dependencies (React 19, Vite 6, Tailwind CSS v4, Framer Motion, Recharts, lucide-react) are well-maintained, widely-used open source libraries with active security response processes. npm audit should be run periodically, particularly before annual content updates. Maintain
No authentication surface The application has no login, no user accounts, and no privileged operations. There is no authentication or authorisation surface to attack. Pass
No server-side code The application is entirely client-side. There is no backend, no database, no API server, and no shell execution surface. Server-side vulnerability classes (SQL injection, command injection, SSRF, etc.) do not apply. Pass

Overall assessment

MU Programme Finder 2026 presents a low security risk profile. Its architecture as a fully static, client-side application with no user accounts and no runtime data fetching eliminates the majority of common web application vulnerability classes. No critical or high-severity issues were identified.

Two items are noted for action:

  1. CSP header — configure if/when the app is moved to MU self-hosting
  2. MU logo — add a local fallback image to public/ for fully offline kiosk use
This review was conducted by inspection of the application source code and build configuration. It is not a formal penetration test. For formal accreditation, a third-party penetration test should be commissioned if the app is deployed in contexts handling sensitive student data.

11 Accessibility

  • Font size toggle — available in the Programme Directory (3 sizes, persisted to localStorage)
  • Keyboard navigable — all interactive elements are focusable
  • Touch-optimised — large tap targets throughout, suitable for iPad kiosk use
  • Reduced motion — animations respect the OS-level prefers-reduced-motion setting via Framer Motion
  • Colour contrast — faculty and category colours selected for reasonable contrast on white backgrounds
  • Help system — persistent help button in the header; first-visit tip on the Welcome screen

12 Known Limitations

  • CAO points data is from 2025 and must be updated annually after Round 1 results are published
  • 5 programme descriptions (MH101, MH201, MH304, MH503, MH804) are AI-generated and should be replaced with official prospectus text when available
  • The PWA service worker caches the app shell but not external programme pages on mu.ie
  • The MU logo is loaded from the MU CDN; a locally hosted copy is needed for fully offline kiosk use
  • The Froebel sub-programmes (MH001–MH004) share a single URL due to the structure of the Froebel department page on mu.ie
  • GitHub Pages does not support custom HTTP headers — a Content Security Policy requires self-hosting
