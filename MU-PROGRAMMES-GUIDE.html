1 Overview

The MU Programme Browser is an extension of the MU Finder that lets you explore Maynooth University's undergraduate programmes as structured curricula — not just as marketing descriptions. It shows the year-by-year module structure for each programme, with direct links into the full module catalogue.

Where the Module Catalogue answers the question "what modules exist?", the Programme Browser answers "what does a student in this programme actually study, year by year?"

Primary audiences: prospective students planning their studies; current students reviewing their programme requirements; academic staff and administrators cross-referencing module offerings with programme structures; Open Day visitors exploring degree options.

Key capabilities

  • Browse all 45 undergraduate programmes across three faculties
  • View the full year-by-year module structure for each programme in a single modal
  • For programmes with multiple degree streams (e.g. Business & Management, Law), switch between streams within the same modal
  • See at a glance which modules are compulsory and which are optional in each year
  • Click any module to open its full details without leaving the programme view

The full hierarchy — scraped directly from apps.maynoothuniversity.ie — is:

  • Programme — e.g. MH101 Bachelor of Arts
  • Year — Year 1, Year 2, Year 3, Year 4
  • Study Pathway (optional) — e.g. Double Major / Major with Minor / Single Major / Minor; only present in years where the programme offers a structural choice
  • Timetable Group — groups of subjects that share scheduling; typically unlabelled where only one group exists
  • Subject — a named academic area (department + credit total + compulsory/optional); each subject has an expandable overview and a module table
  • Module — individual modules within the subject

The Module Detail modal opens on top of the Programme modal — closing it returns you to the same year and pathway tab in the same programme. You never lose your place in the structure.

4 Programme List

The Programme Browser is accessed from the Programmes tab in the MU Finder header. It shows all 45 undergraduate programmes as browsable cards, filterable by faculty. Cards are sorted in ascending CAO code order (MH001 → MH804).

Programme card

Each card shows:

  • CAO code — e.g. MH201, MH101
  • Programme title — the official name (e.g. Bachelor of Science)
  • Faculty — colour-coded dot and abbreviation (FAH / FSE / FSS)
  • Number of degree streams — shown for multi-stream programmes (e.g. "20 streams" for MH404 Business)
  • CAO points — 2025 entry points where available

5 Programme Modal

Clicking a programme card opens a full-screen modal showing the complete year-by-year module structure for that programme, faithfully mirroring the structure on the official MU course catalogue.

Modal header

  • CAO code and programme title
  • Faculty badge — faculty name and colour
  • Stream selector — for multi-stream programmes, a dropdown to switch between degree variants (see Section 6)
  • Total module count for the selected stream
  • MU course finder link — opens the corresponding page on apps.maynoothuniversity.ie for the active stream

Year tabs

The programme structure is divided into years. Click a year tab to view the structure for that year:

Year 1
Year 2
Year 3
Year 4

Tabs are generated dynamically — a three-year programme shows three tabs; a four-year programme shows four.

Year notes

Where the official MU catalogue includes notes for a year (e.g. credit requirements, registration instructions), these appear in an amber note box at the top of the year. Up to 3 notes are shown by default; if there are more, a More / Less toggle reveals or hides the remainder.

Study pathway sub-tabs

Some years within a programme offer a structural choice — for example, MH101 Bachelor of Arts in Years 2 and 3 offers:

Double Major
Major with Minor
Single Major
Minor

These pathway sub-tabs appear below the year tabs when applicable. Each pathway has its own set of subjects and modules, and may carry its own pathway-level notes. Switching pathways updates the subject and module list immediately.

Subject blocks

Within each year (and pathway, where applicable), modules are organised into named subject blocks — the academic areas that make up the programme. Each subject block shows:

  • Subject code — e.g. CS, MH, EE
  • Subject name — e.g. Computer Science, Mathematics
  • ECTS total — total credits for this subject block in this year
  • Compulsory or Optional badge — whether the entire subject block is required
  • Subject overview — a brief description of the subject area drawn from the MU catalogue. The first 100 characters are shown by default; a More / Less toggle reveals the full text. Where the overview contains sub-lists (formatted as -item sequences in the source), these are rendered as proper bullet lists.

6 Multi-stream Programmes

Some CAO codes map to multiple named degree qualifications — for example, a student who enters MH404 may graduate with any one of 20 different named Business degrees depending on their chosen specialism. The Programme Browser handles this with a stream selector dropdown at the top of the programme modal.

Examples of multi-stream CAO codes

CAO codeTitleStreamsExamples
MH404 Business and Management 20 BBS Business and Management, BBA with Placement, BBS International Business, BBS Marketing, BBA Entrepreneurship…
MH409 Business and Languages 14 BBA Business Management and French Studies, BBA Marketing and German Studies, BBA International Business and Spanish…
MH502 BCL Law (Combined Honours) 9 BCL Law and Arts, BCL Law and Business, BCL Law and Accounting, BCL Law and Criminology — each with standard, international, and placement variants
MH212 BSc Science (with Education) 3 Mathematics and Computer Science, Mathematics, Science

Selecting a stream from the dropdown updates the year tabs and module table to show the structure for that specific degree. The CAO code and shared programme title remain visible throughout.

Note on MH404 / MH409: The Business programmes in particular have many streams due to the breadth of specialisms available (International Business, Marketing, Global Cultures, placement year variants, and language combinations). All streams share a common first-year structure; the divergence occurs in later years.

7 Module Rows

Compulsory vs Optional

The compulsory/optional classification operates at the subject block level — i.e. whether the entire subject area is required within the programme structure. Within a subject block, individual modules may additionally carry a Req badge where the MU catalogue marks them as required within their subject.

This distinction is drawn directly from the official MU course structure data. The compulsory/optional status may vary between streams of the same programme.

Modules not in the catalogue

In a small number of cases, a module listed in a programme structure may not yet have a full entry in the MU module catalogue (282 modules could not be found on the official public course pages — typically retired or recently restructured modules). In these cases, the module code and name are shown but the "Full details" link is disabled.

Skill tag indicators

Where a module in the programme has been tagged with Data or Digital skills, this is visible in the module row — giving programme teams a quick view of the skill coverage embedded across their programme's year structure.

8 Drilling into a Module

Clicking a module code, module name, or the Full details button in any programme year table opens the Module Detail modal — the same modal available from the Module Catalogue — stacked on top of the Programme modal.

The Module Detail modal shows the complete official module record:

  • ECTS credits, semester, International flag, UG/PG level
  • Data / Digital / Critical Skills / Capped badges
  • Full overview (content description)
  • Numbered learning outcomes
  • Teaching & Learning hours breakdown (lectures, labs, tutorials, independent study)
  • Assessment components (type, weighting, duration)
  • Supplemental / resit information
  • Link to the official MU timetable

Closing the Module Detail modal (✕ button, Escape, or backdrop click) returns you to the same year tab in the Programme modal — your place in the programme structure is preserved.

9 Module → Programmes (Reverse Link)

Planned feature — not yet implemented. The reverse link data is fully built and available in public/data/module-to-programmes.json, but the UI to display it in the module detail modal has not yet been added.

When implemented, the module detail modal will include an Appears in section at the bottom, showing all programmes that include this module in their official structure. For each programme entry, the following will be shown:

  • CAO code and programme title
  • Year in which the module appears (Year 1, Year 2, etc.)
  • Whether the module is compulsory or optional in that programme

This will be particularly useful for academic staff reviewing cross-programme dependencies — for example, identifying modules that are compulsory across many programmes (changes to these have wide impact) versus modules unique to a single stream.

10 Data Sources

DataSource
Programme list (CAO codes, titles, faculties) Scraped from MU undergraduate listings; titles verified against official MU course system (apps.maynoothuniversity.ie)
CAO code → qualification code mapping MU course search form — POST search by CAO code returns qualification code(s). 45 CAO codes → 104 qualification codes.
Year-by-year module structure (per qualification code) MU course catalogue — TARGET=QS&MODE=VIEW&QUALIFICATION_CODE={code}&YEAR=2026
Module details (credits, assessment, T&L, etc.) MU course catalogue — TARGET=MODULE&MODE=VIEW&MODULE_CODE={code}&YEAR=2026

All programme structure data is for Academic Year 2026 and will be re-scraped annually. The scraping pipeline is fully automated and runs in a single pass covering all 45 programmes.

11 Known Limitations

  • Postgraduate programmes are not included in this phase
  • 265 module codes appear in programme structures but could not be retrieved from the official course catalogue (likely retired or restructured codes); these show name and code only, with no detail modal
  • The compulsory/optional classification is sourced directly from the official MU course catalogue; inaccuracies in the source data will be reflected here
  • Programme structure data is static and reflects the state of the MU catalogue at the time of the last scrape (March 2026); it does not update in real time
  • For programmes with very large numbers of optional modules (e.g. MH101 Bachelor of Arts, which draws from the full arts module catalogue), the module list within optional subject blocks may be long — filtering within the programme modal is planned for a future iteration
  • The reverse link (Appears in) — showing which programmes include a given module — is not yet implemented in the UI (see Section 9)
